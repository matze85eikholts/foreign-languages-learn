01-to wane:
to decrease in power or size; 
(The politician's career waned as the widening gap between his views and 
those of who he represented became more obvious.)

02-rife:
many in number or very common(used for bad things)
(Never believe anything you hear in a politician's speech;
they are rife with inaccuracies and even lies.)

03-a plague:
a serious disease that kills many people
(Sarah wept as she told her children the story of the plague 
that killed her village, leaving only two survivors to continue their traditions.)

04-to rebound:
increase or improve after a low point
(Even after his investors and customers had abandoned him, Max still thought 
his xylophone business was going to make a rebound.)

05-hazy:
unclear
(A typical ten-year-old has only a hazy idea of 
how to manage money and how important it is to do so)

06-to hurl:
to throw forcefully
(Most people simply drop their pennies in the wishing well,
but Max would insist on hurling his coins into the well,
producing a big splash.)

07-forthcoming:
ready or helpful when needed (used with "not" to describe problems)
(Although our education system is suffering badly from a lack of money, 
help from the government hasn't been forthcoming.)

08-morale:
confidence and enthusiasm within a group of people
(The company morale at Yayu has suffered because of the frequent changes of CEOs.)

09-to spearhead:
to lead a change
(After volunteering at a homeless shelter, I decided to spearhead a volunteer 
program at my office to get more of my coworkers involved.)

10-eerie:
mysterious and scary
(As Joel walked through the haunted house, the eerie music made him even more scared
than he already was.)
